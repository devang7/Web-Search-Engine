  • Description
A Virginia native who defeated Theodore Roosevelt and William Taft in the 1912 election, Wilson served two terms from 1913 to 1921. He became the first Southerner elected to office since Zachary Taylor in 1848. Before his election as U.S. president, Wilson served as governor of New Jersey (1911-1913) and president of Princeton University (1902-1910).

As president, Wilson oversaw some of the nation's most progressive policies and was a major supporter of the 19th Amendment, allowing women the right to vote. His legacy also includes the enactment of the Federal Reserve Act in 1913, as well as reintroducing the State of the Union Address, which had not been given by a President to Congress since 1801. Perhaps most notably, he led the country through the First World War and fought vigorously for the creation of the League of Nations.

Autographed photographs of sitting presidents are extremely popular and sought after by collectors of history. This one is a perfect mint example with the largest signature you'll ever find.
