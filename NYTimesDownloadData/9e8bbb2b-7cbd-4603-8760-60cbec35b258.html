  • 1. How many My Alerts may I create?
    Back to top

    You may create up to twenty news alerts and an unlimited number of stock alerts.

  • 2. Can I create a My Alert to be sent when an article is written by a specific writer?
    Back to top

    Yes. Set up a keyword alert that specifically searches for the author's name in the by-line. First visit the My Alerts management page: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Click Create News Alert. On the next page, select Keyword Alert.

    On the following page, you'll be asked to "Define Your Alert." Where it says "WITH ALL of the words or phrases," type the name of the writer exactly as it appears in the byline. For example: if you are interested in creating an alert for articles by Thomas Friedman, type "Thomas L. Friedman," including the quotation marks. Then select your alert delivery schedule, name your alert, and click Preview Alert. Review your alert, and then click Save Alert. Your new alert should be working within 48 hours.

  • 3. Do I need to use quotes with my keywords while setting up an alert?
    Back to top

    You should use quotes around phrases and names that you would like to match exactly as you've typed them. For example: "Maureen Dowd," "Global Warming," "Time Warner," "Czech Republic," "New York University."

  • 4. Do I need to use commas to separate the keywords or phrases I use in My Alerts?
    Back to top

    No, you do not need to use commas. You may use them if you'd like, but they are ignored by the program. For example, both of the following samples will yield the same results:

    "bird watching", birding, birds and "bird watching" birding birds

  • 5. How do I edit one of My Alerts?
    Back to top

    To edit one of your alerts, first visit the My Alerts management page: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Locate the name of the alert you would like to edit, and click the Edit button to the right of that alert.

    Note: If your news alert is matching too many articles, you may want to add additional words or phrases that must be included or excluded for an article to match your news topic.

  • 6. How do I delete one of My Alerts?
    Back to top

    To delete one of your alerts, first visit the My Alerts management page: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Locate the name of the alert you would like to delete, and click the Delete button to the right of that alert. The alert should be deleted within 48 hours.

  • 7. How do I make a news alert more specific?
    Back to top

    My Alerts makes it easy to narrow the topic of a news alert. Just go to My Alerts: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Click Edit. On the edit screen, enter keywords in the fields labeled "AND WITH ANY of the words or phrases" or "AND WITHOUT ANY of the words or phrases" Changes will go into effect within 48 hours.

  • 8. How can I get a news alert to match more articles?
    Back to top

    First go to My Alerts: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Click the Edit button to the right of the alert you want to change. Review your alert criteria on the next page. Do you have terms that narrow your topic? If so, you may want to broaden them, or eliminate them altogether. If you've already tried editing your existing alert, you may want to try deleting your old alert and setting up a new alert.

  • 9. I receive one alert as a weekly digest on a fairly broad news topic, and I've noticed that I often don't receive all the articles on the site that matched my topic. Why?
    Back to top

    Each alert message is limited to 15 article links. If your alert is set as a weekly digest on a broad topic, you will receive links to only the most recent 15 articles. You may want to narrow your topic focus, or change your delivery schedule from weekly to daily.

  • 10. I've created an alert based on a company's ticker symbol but I only seem to receive alerts based on articles in the Business and Technology sections. Why?
    Back to top

    NYTimes.com only assigns company ticker symbols to articles published in the Business and Technology sections. As a result, alerts will only be sent on articles published in these sections.

  • 11. How do I change my news alerts delivery schedule?
    Back to top

    To change your delivery schedule for news alerts, go to My Alerts: nytimes.com/myalerts

    Locate the name of the alert you would like to modify, and click the Edit button to the right of that alert. On the page that follows, scroll down to "Set Your Delivery Schedule" and click the radio button beside the new delivery option, then scroll down and click Continue. On the next page, review your alert and then click Save Alert. Your new preferences should take effect within 48 hours.

  • 12. How can I find a My Alerts article that I missed?
    Back to top

    First, check your Alert Archive. To get to your Alert Archive, go to My Alerts and click Alert Archive next to the alert in question.

    If you don't find the article you're looking for in your Alert Archive, the article didn't match your alert. To create an alert to match a specific article, return to My Alerts and click Create News Alert. Or, to modify your current alert to match a specific article, return to My Alerts and click Edit.

  • 13. How do I find an article that should have matched one of My Alerts but was not sent to me?
    Back to top

    First, check your Alert Archive. To get to your Alert Archive, go to My Alerts and click Alert Archive next to the alert in question.

    If you don't find the article you're looking for in your Alert Archive, the article didn't match your alert. To modify your alert to match a specific article, return to My Alerts and click Edit.

  • 14. I am going on vacation and want to turn My Alerts off without deleting them. What should I do?
    Back to top

    To turn off one or more of your alerts, visit the My Alerts page: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Locate the name of the alert you would like to turn off, and click the Off button to the right of that alert. Your will not receive any articles until you turn the alert back on again.

  • 15. I turned one of my alerts off. How do I turn it back on?
    Back to top

    To turn on one of your alerts, first visit My Alerts: www.nytimes.com/myalerts

    Locate the name of the alert you would like to turn on, and click the On button to the right of that alert. Your alert should be turned back on within 48 hours.

  • 16. I performed a search on NYTimes.com and found articles that fit one of my news alert criteria, but were not sent to me. Why weren't they sent to me?
    Back to top

    Only New York Times content is sent via My Alerts. Content from our blogs and most stories from wire services, such as the Associated Press and Reuters, are not included in the My Alerts e-mails.

  • 17. How can I set up a news alert for a specific phrase in an article as opposed to individual search words?
    Back to top

    When setting up an alert for a specific phrase, use quotation marks around the phrase or group of words you expect to appear in sequence. For example, use quotations around phrases such as "Major League Baseball" or "Congressional hearings." Always use quotes around phrases, names or dates.

  • 18. How do I ask additional questions about My Alerts?
    Back to top

    E-mail your questions or feedback regarding My Alerts to tracker@nytimes.com.
