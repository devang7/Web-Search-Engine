NYT Cooking FAQ

  • 1. Can I view New York Times Cooking on my smartphone or tablet?
    Back to top

    Yes. There is a Cooking app available for any iPad or iPhone with iOS 8.0 or later. For all other devices, you can visit our mobile site at cooking.nytimes.com.

  • 2. How does New York Times Cooking differ from the Dining section?
    Back to top

    Cooking is designed to make cooking easier. It provides inspiration on what to cook as well as helps you find, save and organize recipes. The Dining section publishes feature stories, food news, recipes and more.

  • 3. Does it cost anything to use New York Times Cooking?
    Back to top

    No. Anyone may access unlimited recipes, collections and videos on the website and in the app. To save or rate recipes, you will need to register an account on NYTimes.com.

    Please note that articles in the Dining section that are not part of New York Times Cooking will count toward your monthly limit.

  • 4. How do I save recipes? Where can I find them?
    Back to top

    You can save any recipe from a recipe card or the recipe page. Saved recipes will be available in your recipe box.

    From the website:

    If you hover over a recipe card on the website, a save icon will appear. Click this icon to save the recipe to your recipe box. On a recipe page, the save button is in the top right corner or directly under the recipe title.

    Saved recipes will be available in your recipe box. You can visit your recipe box by clicking Recipe Box on the menu bar at the top of the screen or by opening the recipe drawer with the top left button from anywhere on the site.

    From the app:

    Click Save on a recipe card to save it to your Recipe Box. On a recipe page, the save button is in the top right corner.

  • 5. Do I need to log in to my NYTimes.com account to use the app or website?
    Back to top

    No. You can download the app and browse the website without logging in. However, you will need to log in to rate recipes or save them to your recipe box.

  • 6. How often is Cooking updated?
    Back to top

    The recipes are updated every day.

  • 7. How do I download the Cooking app?
    Back to top

    The New York Times Cooking app is available in the App Store. The app includes all of the recipes and collections featured on the website.

  • 8. Are there Android, Blackberry or Kindle apps for Cooking?
    Back to top

    The Cooking app is currently available only on iPad and iPhone. However, the website may be accessed through any smartphone or tablet.

  • 9. How do I email or share a recipe from the website or app?
    Back to top

    To share a recipe via Facebook or Pinterest, choose one of these tools while on the recipe page. To share a recipe via email or Twitter, click the arrow button in the top right corner of the recipe page, then choose one of these tools.

    From the app:

    To share a recipe or collection with friends via Twitter, Facebook, text message or email, tap the share icon (on iPhone) or Share (on iPad), then choose your sharing tool.

  • 10. How do I print a recipe from the website?
    Back to top

    Click the print button in the top right corner of the recipe page.

  • 11. How do I read and write Notes in the app?
    Back to top

    Currently, you can read Notes in our iOS app, but you cannot create them. To read Notes, tap the icon in the top right of the screen. To create Notes from your iPhone or iPad, scroll to the bottom of the existing Notes and tap the link to open the recipe in Safari.

  • 12. What kind of Notes are you looking for?
    Back to top

    We are interested in your feedback and suggestions about our recipes. We look for Notes that might prove helpful to other readers who are preparing to cook a meal.

    It’s O.K. if you don’t like a certain recipe or the ingredients listed, however, your Notes should offer constructive information for other readers.

    A few things we won't tolerate: personal attacks, obscenity, vulgarity, profanity (including expletives and letters followed by dashes), commercial promotion, impersonations, incoherence and SHOUTING.

  • 13. Why do you moderate Notes?
    Back to top

    We moderate Notes to ensure that NYT Cooking is a reputable space to gather useful information about a recipe.

    We hope to create an atmosphere in which people who use NYT Cooking can rely on Notes as a trustworthy source for critical feedback or tips regarding published recipes.

    While most Notes will be posted expediently if they are on-topic and not abusive, moderating decisions are subjective and may take some time. We will make them as carefully and consistently as we can.

  • 14. Why do I have to register to write a Note?
    Back to top

    We ask you to complete the simple NYTimes.com registration process to ensure that you are a "real" person and that you accept our terms and conditions. The registration process serves to facilitate the development of our online community and ensure that members take responsibility for their comments. For more information, please see our Registration FAQ.

  • 15. How do I change the name displayed on my Notes?
    Back to top

    If you don’t already have a user name, you will be prompted to enter one:

    After doing this, you can change your Cooking user name at any time. Click the settings icon in the top right and select Edit Profile.

    Changing your user name updates how your name is displayed across the Cooking site, including Notes that you’ve already added to recipes.

  • 16. What personal information is displayed when I leave a Note?
    Back to top

    When you leave a Note, your photo and your user name will be displayed with a link to your recipe box. Your email address will not be displayed anywhere on the site, and private notes will be visible only to you.

  • 17. I found an error in the recipe, what do I do?
    Back to top

    We welcome comments and suggestions, or complaints about errors that warrant correction. Messages can be emailed to cookingcare@nytimes.com.

  • 18. How can I save recipes from other websites or my own recipes to my Recipe Box?
    Back to top

    We offer tools on desktop, phone, and all major browsers to help you easily save as you browse recipes across the web. Learn about all of our saving tools on our Tools page.

  • 19. Can I save recipes from other websites or my own recipes to a Collection?
    Back to top

    At this time, non-NYTimes recipes cannot be organized into different folders; however, we are planning this feature for an upcoming release.

  • 20. Is an Evernote account required to save non-NYT recipes to my Recipe Box?
    Back to top

    An Evernote account is not required to save recipes. However, if you’d like to save recipes that aren’t on the web (e.g. that you’ve authored) to your Recipe Box, at this time please create the recipe in Evernote before sync-ing it to Cooking.

  • 21. How do I sync my Evernote and NYT Cooking accounts? Do I need Evernote’s Web Clipper to sync my accounts?
    Back to top

    You can sync your accounts by simply logging into to both NYT Cooking and Evernote and confirming the sync; start by going to the bottom of the Tools page and clicking on the button “Connect With Evernote.”

  • 22. What content is imported from Evernote? Where do my Evernote recipes save to in my Recipe Box?
    Back to top

    We import all of your Evernote notes that Evernote has automatically identified as a recipe (see more information at Evernote Support), into your Recipe Box Evernote folder. If you have clipped NYT Cooking recipes into Evernote, those will sync to your Recipe Box as regular Cooking recipes, rather than into your Recipe Box Evernote folder.

  • 23. How often does my Recipe Box sync with Evernote?
    Back to top

    Our system automatically keeps your Recipe Box up-to-date and imports new recipes every time you save a recipe to your Evernote account.

  • 24. An Evernote item that is not a recipe was imported into my Recipe Box. How do I fix it?
    Back to top

    We import all of your Evernote notes that Evernote has automatically identified as a recipe. If your note was wrongly identified as a recipe, please contact Evernote Support.

  • 25. How do I unlink my Evernote account?
    Back to top

    First, access the Tools page. (On the iPad or iPhone app, tap on the main menu button on the top left of the screen, tap on the last menu item “Settings”, and in the pop up tap on “Tools for Saving Recipes”. On the website, click on menu button on the top left of the screen, and click the “Tools” link at the bottom of the menu.)

    Scroll down to the bottom of the Tools page to find the “Unlink from Evernote” button.

  • 26. I have multiple Evernote accounts. What should I do?
    Back to top

    At this time, we only support linking one Evernote account to your NYT Cooking Recipe Box.

  • 27. Where do my NYT Cooking recipes save to in Evernote?
    Back to top

    In your Evernote account, you can find all your Cooking recipes under the “NYT Cooking” notebook.
