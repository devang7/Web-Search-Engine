iPad

The NYTimes iPad application brings all of your favorite New York Times content to your iPad. You can download it free of charge from the iTunes App Store. Once installed, it delivers the latest news to you via your Wi-Fi or cellular Internet connection so you can take The New York Times with you wherever you go.

Compatible with iPad devices with iOS 7.0 or later. Please be sure to update to iOS 7.0 so that you can download the latest version of the app.

  • 1. Does it cost anything to use the NYTimes iPad app?
    Back to top

    The NYTimes application is free to download and install, and includes some free content However, to get unlimited access to all sections, you must become a digital subscriber. For more information, please see the Digital Subscriptions and Premium Products FAQ.

    Please note that in the absence of a Wi‑Fi connection, the NYTimes iPad application uses your device's cellular network connection to access and download content. Please check with your carrier to see if you will be charged for usage, especially while traveling.

  • 2. Do I need to register with The New York Times to use the NYTimes iPad application?
    Back to top

    No. You can download the NYTimes iPad application and access some content without registering. Saving articles requires registration and full access requires registration and a subscription. If you have already registered with NYTimes.com, you can use the same user name and password to log in to the app. For full access, you must subscribe to a digital package that includes tablet apps.

  • 3. What devices will the NYTimes iPad application work on?
    Back to top

    The NYTimes iPad application works on any iPad device with iOS 7.0 or later. The Pad 2, iPad with Retina Display (iPad 3 and 4) and iPad mini are compatible with iOS 7.0. The latest version of the NYTimes application will not work on any other device. If iOS 7.0 is not available for your iPad, you can continue to use an older version of the app. However, we will not be able to offer you technical support.

  • 4. I purchased a subscription through iTunes but I still cannot access any locked NYTimes content — what should I do?
    Back to top

    If you have already purchased a subscription but still cannot view all content (or locked content), you can easily restore your iTunes purchase through the app.

    First, verify that your purchase was successful — you should have received a confirmation email from iTunes. If you attempt to make the purchase again, iTunes will inform you that you already have a subscription. Next, check that you are using the correct NYTimes.com login information.

    If following the steps above does not resolve the problem, open the NYTimes app and go to Settings by tapping the settings button on the bottom toolbar. Make sure that you are signed in to the correct NYTimes.com account and tap the Restore Existing Subscription button. Restoring your subscription will not result in any additional charges. If the problem persists, you can contact Customer Care at (800) 591-9233.

  • 5. How do I view my subscription details?
    Back to top

    To view your subscription details and benefits, go to the app settings from the right side of the Sections title bar. Your subscription will be listed below your email address. Tap on your subscription for a list of benefits. If you don't see your email address, you will need to log in or create an account.

  • 6. How do I save an article?
    Back to top

    You can save an article by adding it to the Saved for Later list while you are logged in to your NYTimes.com account. You can access saved articles at any time from any of your devices.

    To add an article to the Saved for Later list, open the article and tap the Save icon in the top right corner. To remove an article, tap the Save icon again.

  • 7. Can I look up the definition of a word in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    Yes. To look up the definition of a word, hold your finger down on the word. When you release your finger, tap Define. If you have not yet selected a dictionary for your device, you will see a message that reads, "No definition found." Tap Manage to select the dictionary of your choice.

  • 8. How do I search in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    You can use the NYTimes app to search articles currently available within the app and older articles on NYTimes.com. Tap the Search icon from the top right of any section front. Articles currently available within the app will appear above articles on NYTimes.com. If more results are available, scrolling to the bottom of the search results list will load additional articles. You can tap any title to be taken to the full article. Swiping left or right will move you to the other articles associated with your search.

  • 9. How do I check the version of iOS on my device and the version of my NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    To check the version of iOS on your iPad:

    Open Settings on the device. Tap General, then About. On the About page, scroll to Version. The NYTimes app requires iOS 7.0 or later.

    To check the NYTimes app version:

    Within the NYTimes app, go to the app settings from the right side of the Sections title bar and tap About. The app version must be 3.0 or later to take advantage of the features included with iOS 7.0.

  • 10. I can’t find my NYTimes app – where is it?
    Back to top

    Your NYTimes app is located in the Newsstand folder on your device. If you upgraded your NYTimes app to 3.0 or later and your device has iOS 7.0 or later, your Newsstand folder will have a colorful icon with images of news, travel, art and sports publications.

  • 11. Can I move the NYTimes app out of the Newsstand folder?
    Back to top

    Yes, if you have updated your device's operating system to iOS 9. On iOS 9, Newsstand has been replaced with a regular folder called Newsstand that you can move apps in and out of.

    To move the NYTimes app out of the folder, press and hold it until it begins to wiggle, then drag it to any spot on your home screen. Once you've placed the app on your screen, press the home button on your device to save the changes.

  • 12. How do I turn on breaking news notifications and alerts?
    Back to top

    The NYTimes app provides breaking news notifications which allow you to stay on top of major news events even when the application is closed. You can also receive even more notifications about business, New York, politics and sports. Please note that these additional alerts do not replace breaking news notifications. For example, you may still receive a breaking news alert that is about politics, even if that topic is off in the Additional Alerts section.

    Turning on Breaking News Alerts

    In the NYTimes app, tap the bell icon in the top right corner of your screen. Make sure that Breaking News Alerts are on. Then open the in-device Settings app. Tap Notification Center, then tap NYTimes. Turn on Show on Lock Screen, then select Banners or Alerts. You can also further customize how you receive your alerts here.

    Turning on Additional Alerts

    In the NYTimes app, tap the bell icon in the top right corner of your screen. Make sure that Breaking News Alerts are on. Select the additional alerts of your choice.

  • 13. How do I browse between articles and sections?
    Back to top

    You can scroll through the top articles of each section front you've chosen to include in your main sections list. To expand a section front into a full list of articles in the section, tap the plus sign at the bottom of the screen. To minimize the full list of articles, tap the minus sign at the bottom of the screen. You can also jump to a section by tapping on the section name in the sections list. Note that you can access sections and blogs that appear below "More Sections" in the sections list by tapping on them as well.

    To read an article, tap the headline or anywhere on the introductory text for that article. Tapping the back arrow in the top left of the article view brings you back to the section front. Swiping left and right in the article view brings you to the other articles in that section.

  • 14. How do I edit the list of sections in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    You can control how sections and blogs are ordered and add or remove them from the main sections list by following the instructions below.

    Reordering Sections

    Tap Edit while you are in the Sections list. Press the anchor icon to the right of the section title and slide it up or down until it is in the location of your choice.

    Adding and Removing Sections

    Only the sections above "More Sections" will be available in your main sections list.

    To add a section or blog to your main sections list, go to the Sections list in the NYTimes app and tap Edit. Scroll down to "More Sections" or "Blogs." Tap the name of the section or blog you want to add and then the plus icon. After adding the section, tap Done.  

    To remove a section, go to the Sections list in the NYTimes app and tap Edit. Tap the red minus icon next to the name of the section you want to remove and then tap Remove. After removing the section, tap Done.

  • 15. How do I access blogs?
    Back to top

    By default, blogs are not included in the main sections list. However, you can add them easily. To add a blog, go to the Sections list in the NYTimes app and tap Edit. Scroll to "Blogs." Tap the name of the blog you want to add and then the plus icon. After adding the blog, tap Done. 

  • 16. How do I turn on automatic updates?
    Back to top

    To turn on the Newsstand automatic update feature for the NYTimes app, open Settings on the device, and then tap Store. Scroll down to NYTimes and move the setting to On.

    News content updates will occur early each morning. The app will only update if you are using a Wi-Fi connection and the battery level on your device is not too low. The Newsstand automatic update feature is only available on devices with iOS version 7.0 or later.

  • 17. How do I turn on the refresh automatically feature?
    Back to top

    The Refresh Automatically feature updates the content in the app each time you open the app; updates will also occur periodically while you use the app. To turn this feature on, go to the app settings by tapping the settings button on the right side of the Sections list. Scroll down to Refresh Automatically and move the switch to On.

    If Refresh Automatically is off, you will have to manually update your content.

  • 18. How do I use AirPlay to stream video content from the NYTimes iPad app to an external device?
    Back to top

    You can use your Apple TV (or compatible third-party hardware) to stream video content from the NYTimes app. Your iPad must be using a Wi-Fi connection and your Apple TV must be on the same network.

    When the NYTimes app detects an Apple TV on the network, the AirPlay icon will appear on the video player. Tap the icon and select where you would like the video to play. Please note, AirPlay streaming requires iOS 4.2 or later.

  • 19. My NYTimes iPad app won't load when I try to upgrade to a new version. What should I do?
    Back to top

    If you are experiencing difficulties with an upgrade, please delete the NYTimes app, reboot your device and reinstall the app in iTunes. To remove the app, touch and hold the NYTimes application icon until it begins to wiggle. Tap the X in the corner of the icon, then confirm the deletion in the window that appears. If you are still having problems after deleting and reinstalling the app, please contact us at ipad@nytimes.com.

  • 20. How do I email or share an article from the NYTimes iPad application?
    Back to top

    You can share articles from the NYTimes app. To share an article, open the full article view and tap the share icon in the top right corner.

    If you are using iOS 8.0 and want to use a sharing tool other than those suggested, scroll to the end of the list and tap More. Turn on the additional sharing tool of your choice. Please note that you must be logged into an account on your device for it to appear in this list.

  • 21. How do I read comments on articles in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    You can read comments on some articles in the NYTimes app. Articles with comments will have a comment icon in the upper right corner of the screen. 

    Tap the comment icon to read all comments, NYT Picks or Reader Picks. 

    Note: You cannot submit comments in the NYTimes app. Please visit our website to submit comments.

  • 22. How do I change the size of text in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    To change font size in article pages, open the Settings app on the device. Tap General and then tap Text Size. You can drag the slider to the left to decrease the font size and to the right to increase the font size.

  • 23. Can I use the app if I'm not connected to a Wi-Fi network and I don't have cellular coverage?
    Back to top

    Yes. The NYTimes iPad application was designed to store content locally on your iPad. Once you have completed the sync process, you will be able to access stored and saved content, even if Airplane Mode is activated or you do not have cellular network coverage. Note that only articles and images that have been fully downloaded will be available for offline reading and that no On NYTimes.com search results will be available while you are offline.

  • 24. How do I remove the NYTimes iPad application from my iPad?
    Back to top

    To remove the NYTimes app, follow the same procedure that you'd use for any other iPad application. Touch and hold the NYTimes iPad application icon until it begins to wiggle. Tap the X in the corner of the icon, then confirm the deletion in the window that appears.

  • 25. I don't have iOS 7.0 — can I still download the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    To get the latest features of the NYTimes app, you should use the latest version of iOS. We do not offer support for older versions of the app. However, if you do not upgrade to iOS 7.0, you can download the last version of the NYTimes app that was compatible with your version of iOS by visiting the App Store and installing our app. A message will appear to prompt you to download the latest compatible version.
