    1. Tunisia Army Clashes With Militants in Mountains, Two Killed: Ministry

      Tunisia troops clashed with armed Islamist militants in the remote mountains near the Algerian border on Friday, killing two of them, arresting another and seizing weapons, the defense ministry said.

    2. China Sees Chance of Six-Party Talks With North Korea

      China has not given up hope for a new round of diplomacy with North Korea to prevent Pyongyang making further advances in its weapons program in violation of U.N. resolutions, Foreign Minister Wang Yi said on Friday.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    3. Ecuador's Paraplegic Presidential Candidate Stirs Hope for Disabled

    4. Ecuador Election Could Decide Assange's Continued Asylum

      Whether WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange can stay at the Ecuadorean embassy in London could depend on the winner of Sunday's presidential election in the South American country.

    5. Serbian PM Vucic Accepts Nomination to Run for President

      Serbia's Prime Minister Aleksandar Vucic formally agreed on Friday to be the presidential candidate of his center-right Serbian Progressive Party (SNS) in an election tentatively slated for April.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    6. French Candidate Macron Sparks Protests Over Algeria Remarks

      A few dozen protesters have urged French presidential hopeful Emmanuel Macron to apologize after he called France's colonization of Algeria a "crime against humanity."
      Feb. 17, 2017

    7. Argentine Ex-Military Chief Arrested in Torture, Kidnap Case

      Former Argentine army chief Cesar Milani was arrested Friday for his alleged role in the kidnapping and torture of two men and a woman during the country's military dictatorship.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    8. Fillon Says Staying in French Presidential Race Come What May

      Conservative French presidential candidate Francois Fillon said on Friday he would stay in the race come what may, after saying for weeks that he would step down if he were put under formal investigation over his wife's employment.

      Feb. 17, 2017

    9. Report: Bank of China to Pay Fine in Italy Money-Laundering

      Italian media says the Bank of China has agreed to pay a 600,000-euro ($640,000) fine as part of a plea deal in a 4.5-billion-euro money laundering probe.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    10. Islamic State 'Bureaucrats' Fleeing Stronghold in Syria: Pentagon

      Islamic State administrators and bureaucrats have started to flee the militant group's Syrian stronghold of Raqqa as a U.S.-backed alliance works to isolate the city, a Pentagon official said on Friday.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    2. Food Is Harder to Find in West Mosul, Say Fleeing Iraqis

      Umm Mohammed decided to flee western Mosul after her husband told her the alternative was that they starve.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    3. After New York, London Kicks Off Fashion Week With Eyes on Creativity

      Chunky knits and frilly dresses took over the London catwalk on Friday as the British capital kicked off its leg of the autumn/winter womenswear fashion week calendar.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    4. Car Bomb in Turkey's Southeast Kills Three-Year-Old, Wounds 15: Anadolu

      A three-year-old was killed and fifteen people were wounded on Friday by a car bomb in a southeastern Turkish town, provincial governor Gungor Azim Tuna was quoted as saying by state-run Anadolu news agency.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    5. Bosnia to Appeal UN Court Ruling Clearing Serbia of Genocide

      Bosnia will ask the United Nation's top court to reconsider its 2007 ruling that cleared Serbia of genocide during Bosnia's 1992-95 war.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    6. Car Bomb in Turkish Town Kills 3-Year-Old, Wounds 15 People

      A car bomb attack targeting government lodgings in southeastern Turkey has killed a 3-year-old child and wounded 15 people, an official said.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    7. Poland's PM Szydlo Leaves Hospital a Week After Car Crash

      Poland's prime minister has been released from a hospital in Warsaw where she was treated for a week after suffering minor injuries in a car crash.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    8. Polish Prime Minister Leaves Hospital a Week After Car Crash

      Polish Prime Minister Beata Szydlo left hospital on Friday after suffering minor injuries in a car accident a week ago and said she would attend next Tuesday's government meeting.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    9. Germany's Die Welt: Turkey Has Detained Our Correspondent

      A German newspaper says its correspondent in Turkey has been taken into police custody in connection with his reporting on a hacker attack on the email account of Turkey's energy minister.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    10. EU Official Accuses Polish Minister of Using 'Alternative Facts'

      The number two official at the European Commission accused the Polish foreign minister in a heated exchange on Friday of using "alternative facts" to defend Warsaw's controversial judicial changes that Brussels says threaten the rule of law.
      Feb. 17, 2017

    11. Harsh Hezbollah Words Aim to Draw 'Red Lines' for Trump-Source

      Speeches by Hezbollah's leader this week were aimed at making clear to the new administration of U.S. President Donald Trump that the Lebanese group could strike U.S. interests by hitting Israel, a source familiar with its thinking said on Friday.
      Feb. 17, 2017
