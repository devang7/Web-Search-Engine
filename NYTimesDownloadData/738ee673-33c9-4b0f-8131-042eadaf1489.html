  • 1. What is social login?
    Back to top

    Social login allows you to use your existing login information from another site, such as Facebook or Google, to sign in to NYTimes.com.

    Social login is not required. You can also register using your email address.

  • 2. Can I link an existing New York Times account with a social networking account associated with a different email address?
    Back to top

    Yes. To do so, log in to your New York Times account, visit the My Account area and click Link Facebook or Link Google.

    For more information, seeÂ How do I connect my Facebook or Google account with my New York Times account?

  • 3. Will The New York Times share any information with my social networks?
    Back to top

    We will not share your information or post to your social networks without your permission.

  • 4. Why can't I log into NYTimes mobile apps with my Facebook or Google account?
    Back to top

    Currently, you cannot create an account with social login on New York Times mobile apps. If you have already created an account through social login and would like to log in to our mobile apps, you can do this by resetting your password at www.nytimes.com/forgot. You can then log in with your email address and your new password.

  • 5. How do I connect my Facebook or Google account with my New York Times account?
    Back to top

    There are two ways to connect your Facebook or Google account with your existing New York Times account.

    While logged in to your New York Times account:

    1. Click Log In in the upper right corner of NYTimes.com home page.
    2. Log in to your New York Times account using your existing New York Times email address and password.
    3. After logging in, click your username in the upper right corner of the page and select My Account.
    4. Click Link Facebook or Link Google in the upper right corner of the page.

    5. Enter your Facebook or Google account information in the pop-up window. Click Sign In, then Accept.
    6. A message will appear stating that your account has been linked.

    While on the NYTimes login page:

    1. Go to www.nytimes.com/login, then click Log in with Facebook or Log in with Google.
    2. Enter your Facebook or Google account information in the pop-up window. Click Sign In, then Accept.
    3. If your Facebook or Google email address matches an existing New York Times account, you will be prompted to link your account.
    4. A message will appear stating that your account has been linked.
  • 6. Can I delink my Facebook or Google account from my New York Times account?
    Back to top

    Yes. If you would like to delink your accounts, log in to your New York Times account, visit the My Account area and click Delink.

    If you created an account with a social networking site to access The New York Times, you can still delink your accounts. However, you must reset your password at www.nytimes.com/forgot and log in with your email address and new password to access your New York Times account in the future.

  • 7. I linked my Facebook or Google account with my New York Times account, but I can't log in. What can I do?
    Back to top

    Verify that you are using the Facebook or Google account that you linked with your New York Times account. If you are still unable to access your account, please contact NYTimes Customer Care.
