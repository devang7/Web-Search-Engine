NYTimes iPhone App

The NYTimes iPhone application allows you to enjoy the high-quality journalism of The New York Times on your iPhone, wherever you are. The app has been specifically designed for browsing and navigating articles, blogs, videos and slide shows on the iPhone platform. Download and sync the latest news directly to your device via Wi-Fi or cellular network to take it with you wherever you go. Share articles, photos and videos with your friends through Facebook, Twitter and email and be the first to read about events as soon as they occur with breaking news alerts.

Available in the iTunes App Store.

Compatible with iPhone and iPod Touch devices with iOS 7.0 or later. Please be sure to update to iOS 7.0 so that you can download the latest version of the app.

  • 1. Does it cost anything to use the NYTimes iPhone application?
    Back to top

    The NYTimes application is free to download and install, and includes some free content. However, to get unlimited access to all sections, you must become a digital subscriber. For more information, please see the Digital Subscriptions and Premium Products FAQ.

    Please note that in the absence of a Wi-Fi connection, the NYTimes iPhone application uses your iPhone's cellular network connection to access and download content. Please check with your carrier to see whether you will be charged for usage, especially while traveling.

  • 2. What devices will the NYTimes iPhone application work on?
    Back to top

    The NYTimes iPhone application works on any iPhone or iPod Touch with iOS 7.0 or later. The iPhone 4, iPhone 4S, iPhone 5, iPhone 5C, iPhone 5S and iPod Touch 5th generation are compatible with iOS 7.0. The most up-to-date version of the NYTimes application will not work on any other device. If iOS 7.0 is not available for your iPhone or iPod Touch, you can continue to use an older version of the app. However, we will not be able to offer you technical support.

    See How do I check the version of iOS on my device and the version of my NYTimes app?

    For more information on NYTimes apps available for other mobile devices, refer to the Mobile Help section of NYTimes.com.

  • 3. I purchased a subscription through iTunes but I still cannot access any locked NYTimes content — what should I do?
    Back to top

    If you have already purchased a subscription but still cannot view all content (or locked content), you can easily restore your iTunes purchase through the app.

    First, verify that your purchase was successful — you should have received a confirmation email from iTunes. If you attempt to make the purchase again, iTunes will inform you that you already have a subscription. Next, check that you are using the correct NYTimes.com login information.

    If following the steps above does not resolve the problem, open the NYTimes app and tap Settings from the Sections list. Make sure that you are signed in to the correct NYTimes.com account and tap Manage iTunes Subscription. You will be able to review your subscription information here. If the problem persists, you can contact Customer Care at (800) 591-9233.

  • 4. How do I view my subscription details?
    Back to top

    To view your subscription details and benefits, tap the Settings button on the right side of the Sections list. Your subscription details will be listed below your email address. Tap on your email address and subscription for a list of benefits. If you don't see your email address, you will need to log in or create an account.

  • 5. How do I get the NYTimes iPhone application?
    Back to top

    You can get the NYTimes iPhone application for free from the App Store on your iPhone or iPod Touch. You can also download it to your computer through the App Store in iTunes: http://www.appstore.com/nytimes. In order to use the App Store, you may need to upgrade to the latest version of the operating system, and the latest version of iTunes. The NYTimes app requires iOS 7.0 or later.

  • 6. How do I search in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    You can use the NYTimes app to search articles currently available within the app and older articles on NYTimes.com. The Search field is at the top of the Sections list, directly above Top News. Search results will appear as you enter your search term. Articles currently available within the app will appear above articles on NYTimes.com. If more results are available, scrolling to the bottom of the search results list will load additional articles. You can tap any title to be taken to the full article. Swiping left or right will move you to the other articles associated with your search.

  • 7. How do I check the version of iOS on my device and the version of my NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    To check the version of iOS on your iPhone:

    Open the Settings app on the device, tap General, then About. On the About page, scroll to Version. The NYTimes app requires iOS 7.0 or later.

    To check the NYTimes app version:

    Within the NYTimes app, go to the app settings from the right side of the Sections title bar and tap About. The app version must be 4.0 or later to take advantage of the features included with iOS 7.0.

  • 8. I can’t find my NYTimes app — where is it?
    Back to top

    Your NYTimes app is located in the Newsstand folder on your device. If you upgraded your NYTimes app to 4.0 or later and your device has iOS 7.0 or later, your Newsstand folder will have a colorful icon with images of news, travel, art and sports publications.

  • 9. Can I move the NYTimes app out of Newsstand?
    Back to top

    Yes, if you have updated your device's operating system to iOS 9. On iOS 9, Newsstand has been replaced with a regular folder called Newsstand that you can move apps in and out of.

    To move the NYTimes app out of the folder, press and hold it until it begins to wiggle, then drag it to any spot on your home screen. Once you've placed the app on your screen, press the home button on your device to save the changes.

  • 10. How do I turn on breaking news notifications and alerts?
    Back to top

    The NYTimes app provides breaking news notifications which allow you to stay on top of major news events even when the application is closed. You can also receive even more notifications about business, New York, politics and sports. Please note that these additional alerts do not replace breaking news notifications. For example, you may still receive a breaking news alert that is about politics, even if that topic is off in the Additional Alerts section.

    Turning on Breaking News Alerts

    In the NYTimes app, tap the bell icon in the top right corner of your screen. Make sure that Breaking News Alerts are on. Then open the in-device Settings app. Tap Notification Center, then tap NYTimes. Turn on Show on Lock Screen, then select Banners or Alerts. You can also further customize how you receive your alerts here.

    Turning on Additional Alerts

    In the NYTimes app, tap the bell icon in the top right corner of your screen. Make sure that Breaking News Alerts are on. Select the additional alerts of your choice.

  • 11. How do I save an article?
    Back to top

    You can save an article by adding it to the Saved for Later list while you are logged in to your NYTimes.com account. You can access saved articles at any time from any of your devices.

    To add an article to the Saved for Later list, open the article and tap the Save icon in the top right corner. To remove an article, tap the Save icon again. You can also remove items using the Edit button while viewing the Saved for Later list.

  • 12. Can I look up the definition of a word in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    Yes. To look up the definition of a word, hold your finger down on the word. When you release your finger, tap Define. If you have not yet selected a dictionary for your device, you will see a message that reads, "No definition found." Tap Manage to select the dictionary of your choice.

  • 13. My NYTimes iPhone app won't load when I try to upgrade to a new version. What should I do?
    Back to top

    If you are experiencing difficulties with an upgrade, please delete the NYTimes app, reboot your device and reinstall the app in iTunes. To remove the app, touch and hold the NYTimes application icon until it begins to wiggle. Tap the X in the corner of the icon, then confirm the deletion in the window that appears. If you are still having problems after deleting and reinstalling the app, please contact us at iphone@nytimes.com.

  • 14. How do I update content?
    Back to top

    Content will be updated automatically early each morning, as well as each time you open the app and periodically while you use the app.

    To update manually, pull down on any section front. You must leave the NYTimes app open while your content is updating. If the connection is interrupted for any reason, either to use another application or to receive a phone call, you may need to relaunch the app and restart.

  • 15. How do I edit the list of sections and blogs in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    You can control how sections and blogs are ordered and add or remove them from the Sections list by following the instructions below.

    Reordering Sections

    Tap Edit while you are in the Sections list. Press the anchor icon to the right of the section title and slide it up or down until it is in the location of your choice.

    Adding and Removing Sections

    Only the sections in your Sections list will be available on your device. This is helpful if there's a section that you never read: removing it from the Sections list will prevent the NYTimes app from refreshing that content during the update process. You can add a section back at any time. Please note, by default blogs are not included in the app. You can add a blog to the Sections list the same way you would add a section.

    To add a section or blog to the Sections list, go to the Sections list and tap Edit, then the plus icon. You can switch between a list of all available Sections and Blogs by tapping the Blogs or Sections tab on the top toolbar. Tap the name of the section or blog you want to add and then tap Done.  

    To remove a section, go to the Sections list in the NYTimes app and tap Edit. Tap the red minus icon next to the name of the section you want to remove and then tap Remove. After removing the section, tap Done.

  • 16. How do I email or share an article?
    Back to top

    You can share articles from the NYTimes app. To share an article, open the full article view and tap the share icon in the top right corner.

    If you are using iOS 8.0 and want to use a sharing tool other than those suggested, scroll to the end of the list and tap More. Turn on the additional sharing tool of your choice. Please note that you must be logged into an account on your device for it to appear in this list.

  • 17. How do I turn on the refresh automatically feature?
    Back to top

    The Refresh Automatically feature updates the content in the app each time you open the app; updates will also occur periodically while you use the app. To turn this feature on, go to the app settings by tapping the settings button on the right side of the Sections list. Scroll down to Refresh Automatically and move the switch to On.

    If Refresh Automatically is off, you will have to manually update your content.

  • 18. How do I use AirPlay to stream video content from the NYTimes iPhone app to an external device?
    Back to top

    You can use your Apple TV (or compatible third-party hardware) to stream video content from the NYTimes app. Your iPhone must be using a Wi-Fi connection and your Apple TV must be on the same network.

    When the NYTimes app detects an Apple TV on the network, the AirPlay icon will appear on the video player. Tap the icon and select where you would like the video to play. Please note, AirPlay streaming requires iOS 4.2 or later.

  • 19. Can I use the app if I'm not connected to a Wi-Fi network and I don't have cellular coverage?
    Back to top

    Yes. The NYTimes iPhone application was designed to store content locally on your iPhone. Once you have completed the sync process, you will be able to access stored and saved content even if Airplane Mode is activated, or you do not have cellular network coverage. Note that only articles that have been fully downloaded will be available for offline reading and that no On NYTimes.com search results will be available while you are offline.

  • 20. How do I read comments on articles in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    You can read comments on some articles in the NYTimes app. Articles with comments will have a comment icon in the upper right corner of the screen. 

    Tap the comment icon to read all comments, NYT Picks or Reader Picks. 

    Note: You cannot submit comments in the NYTimes app. Please visit our website to submit comments.

  • 21. How do I change the size of text in the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    To change the font size in section fronts and article pages, open the Settings app on the device. Tap General and then tap Text Size. You can drag the slider to the left to decrease the font size and to the right to increase the font size.

  • 22. How do I remove the NYTimes iPhone application from my iPhone?
    Back to top

    To remove the NYTimes app, follow the same procedure that you'd use for any other iPhone application. Touch and hold the NYTimes application icon until it begins to wiggle. Tap the X in the corner of the icon, then confirm the deletion in the window that appears.

  • 23. Do I need to register for NYTimes.com to use the iPhone application?
    Back to top

    No. You can download the NYTimes iPhone application and access some content without registering. However, full access requires registration and a subscription. If you have already registered with NYTimes.com, you can use the same user name and password to log in to the app. For full access, you must subscribe to a digital package that includes smartphone apps.

  • 24. I don't have iOS 7.0 — can I still download the NYTimes app?
    Back to top

    To get the latest features of the NYTimes app, you should use the latest version of iOS. We do not offer support for older versions of the app. However, if you do not upgrade to iOS 7.0, you can download the last version of the NYTimes app that was compatible with your version of iOS by visiting the App Store and installing our app. A message will appear to prompt you to download the latest compatible version.
