Saving Stories: How Does It Work? 

If you are logged in on nytimes.com, you can save Times stories to read later. Saved stories appear in your Reading List, which is accessible on your computer, smartphone or tablet.

  • 1. How do I save?
  • 2. How do I find my stories?
  • 3. How do I unsave (remove) stories?
  • 4. How do I send questions or feedback?
  • 1. How do I save?
    Back to top
    • From a desktop or a laptop, first open the story you want to save. Then tap the bookmark icon in the top-right corner. (To remove the story, tap the icon again.)
    • In The Times’s mobile apps, you can save a story from the home screen by tapping the bookmark icon. On the article screen, in the iPhone app, tap the bookmark icon to save; Android app users can tap the the three dots at the top-right of the screen and select the save option.
    • In mobile browsers, you can save a story from the home screen by tapping Read Later. On an article page, tap the circle icon under the article’s headline and select the save option.

    Remember, you must be a subscriber or a registered user with The New York Times to access this feature. Register here.

  • 2. How do I find my stories?
    Back to top

    Some readers will see their Reading List on the home page. If you do, the last three articles you saved will appear in your Reading List for immediate access.

    For all readers, all saved stories can be found at nytimes.com/saved.

    In the mobile apps, retrieve your stories by opening Saved for Later from the section menu.

    Regardless of what device you used to save items, they will be retrievable on any device.

  • 3. How do I unsave (remove) stories?
    Back to top

    You can unsave (remove) a previously saved story in one of two ways.

    • Open Reading List in a web browser. If you're on a computer, hover over the story you wish to unsave, then click the X button. If you’re on a smartphone, tap Edit, then tap the X button next to the story you wish to unsave.
    • Open the article. Tap the bookmark icon a second time to unsave.
  • 4. How do I send questions or feedback?
    Back to top

    We would welcome any thoughts you have on this feature. Please take a brief survey, or send your suggestions to The Times at readinglist-feedback@nytimes.com.
