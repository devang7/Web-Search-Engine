Help With Downloaded ebooks

Downloaded ebooks can be read on any compatible e-reading device. After you purchase an ebook, carefully follow the instructions on that ebook’s page. Ebooks downloaded to your computer will not automatically sync with your device.

iPad, iPhone

If you are on your iPad or iPhone (recommended):

  • Make sure you have the iBooks App installed on your iPhone or iPad. (It does not come pre-installed on iPhones.) Need the iBooks App?
  • Download the ebook and open it in the iBooks app when prompted.

If you are on your computer:

  • Download the .epub ebook file to your computer.
  • Drag the .epub file from your computer to iTunes. (If you have upgraded to OS X Mavericks, you will be unable to load the ebook from iTunes. You can either read the ebook on the iBooks desktop app or switch to your iOS device.
  • Plug your iPad or iPhone into your computer.
  • In iTunes, go into the "books" section and drag the ebook to your iPhone or iPad.

Nook

  • Download the .epub ebook file to your computer.
  • Connect the Nook to your computer and access it in Finder (Mac) or Windows Explorer (PC).
  • Locate the folder: My Nook > MyFiles > Books.
  • Drag and drop the .epub file to that folder.
  • Search your Nook Library for the ebook’s title.

Kindle

  • Download the .epub ebook file to your computer.
  • Connect the Kindle to your computer.
  • The Kindle will load as a storage device called "kindle" on the Desktop (Mac) or My Computer (PC).
  • Open the "documents" folder located inside the "kindle" storage device.
  • Drag and drop the .mobi file to the “documents” folder on your Kindle.

Android

  • Download the .epub e-ebook file to your Android
  • Install the Aldiko Book Reader App on your Android for free.
  • Open the link provided on your Android’s web browser (the popup browser will not work).
  • In the Aldiko App, go to "Files" > "Download" > find the .epub file > "Import".

Computer

  • Download the .epub ebook file to your computer.
  • Install Adobe Digital Editions; it’s free and our recommended reader.
  • When the .epub file is finished downloading, open it with Adobe Digital Editions.

Web Reader Preview Troubleshooting

Choose a modern web browser

Book's Web Reader allows you to preview your ebook online in any modern web browser. However, we recommend Google Chrome for the best reading experience.

You can download Chrome here: https://www.google.com/chrome

Update your browser

The reader requires that you use a recent version of your browser.

Google Chrome (Mac / Windows): Use the latest version.

Chrome updates automatically. If you've encountered an error during an update, please visit Chrome's support page: https://support.google.com/chrome/answer/111996

Safari OS X (Mac): Use the latest version.

To check for Safari updates, go to the main Apple menu and select "Software Update."
See how here: http://support.apple.com/kb/HT1338

Safari iOS (iPad / iPhone): Use the latest version.

On your iPad / iPhone, go to Settings > General > Software Update to check for updates.
See how here: http://support.apple.com/kb/HT4623

Firefox (Mac / Windows): Use the latest version.

Firefox automatically updates by default, but if you have declined automatic updates, you can check for newer versions here:
http://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/update-firefox-latest-version

Internet Explorer (Windows): Use one of the two latest versions.

Download the most recent version of IE here:

http://www.microsoft.com/en-us/download/ie.aspx?q=internet+explorer

Android Browser: Use Android 2.3 and up.

The Android browser can only be updated if the device/carrier offers an update to the operating system.

Switch to a faster network connection

Ebook previews may take some time to load over a slow Internet connection. If a preview takes too long to load over a cellular broadband network on a mobile device, switch to wifi for better results. If you're connecting over wifi, try switching to a faster wifi network or a local-area network for better results.

Restart your browser, reset your browser's cache

It may be possible to resolve lingering technical issues by restarting your web browser, or by resetting your browser's cache. Here's how to reset your cache on our supported browsers:

  • Google Chrome: http://support.google.com/chrome/bin/answer.py?hl=en&answer=95582
  • Safari (OS X): http://support.apple.com/kb/PH11914
  • Safari (iOS): http://support.apple.com/kb/ht1677
  • Firefox: http://support.mozilla.org/en-US/kb/how-clear-firefox-cache
  • Internet Explorer: http://support.microsoft.com/kb/260897
  • Android browser: http://www.accessc.com/articles/clear-history-cookies-cache-android.html

Contact Support

If the above troubleshooting tips do not work for you or if you have a different problem, please contact tbookcare@nytimes.com. The more information you can provide, the faster we'll be able to diagnose and fix your problem.
